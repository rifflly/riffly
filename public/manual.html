1 Install the app

Riffly lives at one web address — rifflly.github.io/riffly. It works right in your browser, but installing it adds an icon to your home screen, opens full-screen, and works offline.

🤖 Android (Chrome)

  1. Open Chrome and go to rifflly.github.io/riffly.
  2. Tap the ⋮ menu (top-right).
  3. Tap Add to Home screen (or Install app) → Add.

🍎 iPhone / iPad (Safari)

  1. Open Safari and go to rifflly.github.io/riffly.
  2. Tap the Share button (square with an up-arrow).
  3. Scroll down → Add to Home Screen → Add.

💻 Computer (Chrome / Edge)

  1. Go to rifflly.github.io/riffly.
  2. Click the install icon in the address bar → Install.
💡 Works offline Once installed, Riffly is saved on your device and opens even with no internet.

2 First-time setup

Along the bottom are six tabs: Learn · Chords · Play · Songs · Ask · Settings. Tap between them any time.

Turn on sound (once per visit)

Phones only allow sound after you tap something. When you see a Tap to enable sound bar, tap Enable. Now the metronome, tuner, and songs can play. You do this once each time you open Riffly.

Left- or right-handed

Left-handed? Go to Settings → Left-handed mode and switch it on. Every chord diagram flips to match your view.

3 Learn — lessons & rewards

The Learn tab is your step-by-step path from your very first note to whole songs — 18 lessons across 5 stages. At the top you'll see the cover banner, your progress, and your practice rewards.

  1. Tap the Learn tab, then tap any lesson to open it.
  2. Read each step and try it on your guitar. Some lessons have built-in tools — a metronome, chord-change trainer, or strumming player. Tap Start inside them to play along.
  3. When you're done, tap “I did it! ✓” — you'll get a little celebration, and the lesson gets a green tick.

Your rewards (streak, mission, badges)

  • 🔥 Daily streak — grows by one each day you practise anything (a lesson, a chord switch, a song, or the metronome). Miss a day and it restarts — so a few minutes daily is the goal.
  • Today's mission — a small daily challenge (e.g. “Finish one lesson”). It ticks off automatically when you do it.
  • Weekly goal — the dots fill in as you practise on different days; aim for 4 days a week.
  • Badges — earn them as you go: First Win, Smooth Switch, Song Starter, Rhythm Rookie, 3-Day Streak, Chord Climber. Locked ones show a 🔒.
📌 Graduation Finish all 18 lessons and you'll get a “You did it!” card. Tap the floating 💬 Ask button inside a lesson to ask the tutor about that exact lesson.

4 Tuner — get in tune

An out-of-tune guitar sounds wrong no matter how well you play. The tuner listens through your mic — nothing is recorded or sent anywhere.

  1. On the Learn tab, tap the “Tune your guitar” card at the top.
  2. Tap Start tuning and allow the microphone.
  3. Pluck one string — Riffly shows which string it is and a needle.
  4. Turn the tuning peg until the needle sits in the middle and it says “In tune! ✓”.
  5. Repeat for all six strings.
💡 No mic, or noisy room? Tap a string name (E A D G B e) to hear its reference tone and tune by ear.

5 Chords — the library

The Chords tab is your reference book of chord shapes.

  1. Tap the Chords tab.
  2. Tap any chord to see a big diagram and a beginner tip.
  3. New to all this? Tap “New to chords? Start here” at the top for a friendly one-minute explainer — what a chord is, major vs minor, and how Riffly builds you up.

6 Play — practice tools

The Play tab has three tools. Tap the switcher at the top to move between them.

Metronome

A steady click to keep your timing. Set the speed with the slider or Tap tempo, choose beats per bar, and press Start.

Chord Changes

Practise switching between two chords cleanly. Pick a pair, press Start, and change to the highlighted chord each bar. Try the one-minute challenge to score your clean changes and beat your best.

Rhythm

A strumming + drum backing to play along with. Pick a groove, choose a chord, set the tempo, and press Play.

📌 It all counts Practising here builds your streak, ticks off today's mission, and can unlock badges.

7 Songs — the library

The Songs tab is your play-along library, grouped by difficulty so you always know what to try next:

  • Songs You Can Play Now — two or three easy chords.
  • Songs to Level Up — adds C, G, D; includes the starter singalongs.
  • Challenge Songs — barre chords and faster changes.
  • Hindi Songs to Try — familiar desi tunes and jams.
  1. Tap a starter song to open the studio and play along; tap a mission card to see its chord shapes and a practice goal.
  2. Choose a View (Play / Sing / Both) and a Backing (App Rhythm / Silent), then press Play. Riffly counts you in and scrolls the song in time.
  3. Add your own: Import a chord sheet, or tap + New song to build one by hand.

8 Play along with your own music

Add a real recording and play along — with a waveform, a loop tool, a pitch-perfect slow-down, and a one-tap Reduce vocals for singing.

  1. On the Songs tab, tap 🎵 Add your music (MP3) and choose a file (MP3, M4A, WAV). It's saved on your device and opens playing straight away. (Or open any song → Backing → My Audio.)
  2. Loop a section: drag the two handles on the waveform, then turn on Loop a section.
  3. Slow it down: drag the speed slider (50–100%). Same key, no chipmunk voices.

🎤 Sing it or play it yourself

Want to sing? Turn on Reduce vocals — it quietens the singing so you can take over. Works best on stereo songs; it isn't perfect.

Want a cleaner result, or to remove an instrument (like the guitar) to play it yourself? Tap “Mute vocals or an instrument, cleanly →”. Riffly plays your file as-is, so you prep it once with a free tool, download the result, then tap Replace. The guide walks you through both “Sing it myself” and “Play an instrument myself”.

⚠️ Quality varies by song Removing vocals is usually cleaner than isolating one instrument — that's normal.

9 Ask — the AI tutor

The Ask tab is an optional chat helper that answers guitar questions in plain, beginner-friendly language. The whole app works fine without it. You give Riffly a free key from Google or Groq (next section). Your key stays only on your device — Riffly has no server and never sees it.

  1. Tap the Ask tab and type a question — like “How often should a beginner practise?” — then tap Send.
  2. 🎤 Speak instead of typing: tap the microphone, allow it once, and say your question. Edit if you like, then Send.
  3. Ask follow-ups any time. Inside a lesson, the floating Ask button fills in that lesson's context for you.

10 Getting your free AI key

A “key” is just a long password that lets Riffly talk to a free AI service on your behalf. Pick one of the two.

Option A — Google Gemini (recommended)

  1. Go to aistudio.google.com/app/apikey and sign in with a Google account.
  2. Tap Create API key (accept the default project if asked).
  3. Copy the key (starts with AIza…). You don't need billing — the free tier is enough.
  4. In Riffly → Ask tab, make sure Google Gemini is selected, paste the key, and tap Save & start chatting.

Option B — Groq

  1. Go to console.groq.com/keys and sign up (free).
  2. Tap Create API Key, then copy it right away (shown once).
  3. In Riffly → Ask → Groq, paste the key, and tap Save & start chatting.
📌 If you hit a free limit Riffly tells you which kind: “Try again in N seconds” means wait a moment; “That's today's free limit” resets around midnight US Pacific — or tap Switch provider to hop to the other service. A Groq key is a handy backup since its limits are separate.
⚠️ “Key was rejected”? The key usually wasn't copied in full. Copy it again with the copy icon and re-paste.

11 Settings & backups

  • Sound — turn sound on if it isn't already.
  • Left-handed mode — flips all chord diagrams.
  • Your data → Back up my data — saves a file with your progress, streak, badges, songs, and settings.
  • Restore from backup — load that file on this or a new device to bring everything back.
  • Backing tracks — see and remove uploaded audio.
  • Version number — check you're up to date.
💡 New phone? Back up on the old one, email yourself the file, then Restore on the new one.

12 Tips & troubleshooting

  • I don't see a new feature — if a “New version ready — tap to refresh” bar appears, tap it. Otherwise close the app fully and reopen.
  • No sound — tap Enable on the “Tap to enable sound” bar (once per visit).
  • Tuner/mic not working — allow the microphone when asked (iPhone: Settings → Safari → Microphone).
  • Reduce vocals still has some singing — that's normal for this quick trick; use “Mute vocals or an instrument, cleanly →” for a cleaner result.
  • Tutor says it's resting — you've hit a free daily limit; try again later or switch provider.
